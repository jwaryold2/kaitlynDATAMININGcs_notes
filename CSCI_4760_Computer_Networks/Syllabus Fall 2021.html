COURSE DESCRIPTION

This Course will cover the fundamental of Computer Networking and internet.  The course will cover Chapter 1-7 and some part of Chapter 8 from the textbook. A topical list of the material is as follows:

 

Computer Networks and Internet (Chapter 1) 

Network Applications, HTTP, FTP (Chapter 2)

TCP and UDP (Chapter 3)

Forwarding and Routing (Chapter 4,5)

Link Layer and LAN(Chapter 6)

Introduction to Wireless Networks (Chapter 7)

Introduction to computer security (Chapter 8)

Pre-Requisites and/or Co-Requisites

  • CSCI 2720 – Data Structures
  • And CSEE 2670 – Introduction to Theory of Computing or CSEE 2220

COURSE OBJECTIVES

By the conclusion of this course, students will learn:

  1. networking concepts, protocols and understand how internet and computer network works.

Textbook

  • J. F. Kurose and K. W. Ross, Computer Networking: A Top-Down Approach, 7th Edition

COURSE Delivery Mode

This is a face-to-face class so class will not be recorded and all the students are required to attend the class.  Be sure to check due dates and times for assignments, quizzes and exams.

Homework

There is a homework assignment for each module  for a total of 5 homework assignments (Module 6 does not have homework). Each student is expected to do his/her own work. Teamwork is not allowed. Homework is due on the specified day at the specified time. Late work is not accepted. 

Exams and quizzes

There will be one quiz for each Module for a total of 6 quizzes. The tests/quizzes will be held during the class period on the scheduled date.
No make-up quiz. Under special, unforeseen circumstances that are clearly document-able , Average of quizzes will be used as a replacement for the missed quiz.
The  exam 1 will be on  Thursday February 29.  The exam 2 will be on TMonday May 6, 12:00 - 2:00 . Exams are closed book . Students are expected to familiarize themselves with the academic honesty policy of the University of Georgia. 

Programming Assignments

There will be a total of 3 programming assignments in this course. These assignments are due on the specified day at the specified time. No late work will be accepted. Each student is expected to do his/her own work.
